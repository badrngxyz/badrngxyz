# Nothing

I considered saying "mistakes," but that sets an expectation, and if I expect to knowingly mess up, it won't work. 😅

If happiness equals reality minus expectations, wouldn't lowering expectations while simultaneously improving reality increase happiness? Expectations differ from anticipation: one creates pressure, while the other promotes eagerness. Therefore, I look forward to creating, sharing, learning, and acting on my dreams without feeling discouraged when reality throws a curveball.

That's the ideal: to prioritize progress over perfection. Holding back until perfection arrives leads to stagnation, while acknowledging mistakes and progressing fuels momentum. However, there's always a barrier to overcome. 🥲 I wish I could say the fear of inaction finally outweighed the fear of failure, but that's only partially true. Ironically, finding the perfect domain name kick-started everything! 🤣

# Bad

Quality is subjective. Instead of trying to please everyone and delaying release out of fear of criticism, I'll focus on doing my best with my current knowledge. I'll prioritize releasing over achieving absolute flawlessness. Done is better than perfect!

Above all, I want to create for myself, document my learning journey, and continuously improve through revisions. It's only "bad" if I allow it to be, and it can never be "good" if I don't start. Therefore, I may make mistakes, but I'll embrace feedback from readers to improve.

# Random

Creativity thrives on diversity, so why limit myself to a single subject? I'm a software developer, and even within that field, there's a vast range of specializations. Also, incorporating other disciplines allows me to draw richer analogies.

Drawing inspiration from nature, or anything that undergoes evolution, offers shortcuts to validating ideas. Years of testing prune away unnecessary elements, so drawing parallels allows me to leverage that extensive process.

Therefore, the more I tinker and learn across different areas, the greater my exposure to useful insights. I won't claim expertise in anything, but I want to avoid confining myself to a single subject and stifling other interests. If I do anything interesting and can ramble about it, it'll likely end up here. 🫣

# Progress

I don't know where I'm going, but does that mean there can't be progress along the way? If I improve by 0.1% in any direction, isn't that progress? It might take years to reach a significant milestone, but for me, progress has never been about designing the ideal future. It's often been about reflecting on the past and seeing how far I've come. If I need to distance myself from the past to progress, I must first move!

That's the beauty of xyz: letters often representing variables we can adjust. I don't yet know the equation they belong to, but I hope to discover it while experimenting with them. It's in uncertainty that discovery lies, so trying things out while remaining open to changing course provides a greater chance of progress than simply aiming for a straight line.
